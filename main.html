Our Work

The usage of banned weapons is classified as a crime against humanity. Hence, the aim of the research is to introduce other ways of classifying munitions in order to introduce a method for proving the usage of such weapons and therefore impose sanctions on the responsible parties. It may also be of use for monitoring the decisions taken on conventions.

Logistic regression for image classification

The weapon images are classified from one another with logistic regression. The logistic regression algorithm is first iterated through the training dataset (the images in it are labeled, as ‘cluster’, ‘grad’ as the first experiment was deriving the cluster munitions from BM-21 Grad rockets).

Logistic regression for text classification

The misinformation about emigration is also becoming more pressing, as many civilians are seeking shelter abroad. Hence, a model for processing text messages analyzing the road situation and the country's exits is introduced, the aim of it is to keep the migrants informed of the conditions. The model uses several algorithms: logistic regression with an automatic process for labeling data such as negative, neutral, positive (sentiment analysis)

Long short-term memory

The LSTM analyses the words as individual ones, part of a string with no connections between them, hence it does not output satisfactory accuracy of the validation dataset.

Forbidden weapons:

In modern war addition to the many infrastructural and human losses, is that sometimes some of the used weapons by one/both sides include weapons banned by United Nations (UN) conventions. Such weapons are:


  • Cluster munitions

  • Usage: From 2010 to 2019 at least 4,315 cluster munitions
  • Where? 80 percent are in Syria
  • How about Russia? Weapons investigators say Russian use of cluster munitions across Ukraine
  • Increase: There at least 286 people have been killed or wounded in 2019 from cluster-bomb attacks or from remnants of the munitions, compared to 149 in 2018, according to the NY Times.

  • Poisonous gases

  • Types: nerve, choking, nettle, phosgene
  • Nerve gases: break down the neurotransmitters that make organs function
  • Choking gases: impedes the victim’s ability to breathe, causing a buildup of fluid in the lungs
  • Phosgene: Severe respiratory effects, including pulmonary edema, pulmonary emphysema, and death have been reported in humans.

  • Mines

  • Banned by: The Anti-Personnel Mine Ban Convention
  • Casualties: The vast majority - 79% - are civilians
  • Child casualties: over 46% of the overall number
  • Endless Support
  • Countries:more than three-quarters of the world's countries (162 States) have joined the Convention
